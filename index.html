  • Interest Calculation: The interest on a student loan is calculated based on the outstanding loan amount and the interest rate. The interest is typically calculated on a daily or monthly reducing balance, which means that the interest is charged only on the outstanding principal amount and not on the entire loan amount.

Eligibility Ceriteria of Fedaral Bank

Here are the general eligibility criteria for a student loan in India:

  • You should be an Indian citizen to apply for a student loan..
  • You should be above 18 years of age to be eligible for a student loan. In some cases, banks may require the borrower to be between 18 to 35 years of age.
  • You should have secured admission to a recognized course in India or abroad. The course could be a graduate, post-graduate, diploma, or professional course.
  • The institute and course should be recognized by a competent authority. Banks may have their own list of approved institutes and courses, so it's important to check with the bank before applying.
  • Most banks require a co-borrower for a student loan, who could be your parent, guardian, or spouse. The co-borrower should have a regular source of income and a good credit history.
  • Banks may require collateral or security to approve a student loan, especially for high loan amounts. The collateral could be a house, property, fixed deposit, or any other asset of equivalent value. Some banks may also offer unsecured loans for certain courses or based on the borrower's profile.
  • The borrower and the co-borrower should have a good credit score, which indicates their creditworthiness and repayment capacity. A good credit score increases the chances of loan approval and may also result in a lower interest rate.
